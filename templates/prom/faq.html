Fequently asked game questions - Written by Nuclear Raunch of Frost and Flame Promisance

Q: What is the normal amount of acceptable attacks on an empire before it is considered an act of aggression?
A: The general rule is not more than 7 attacks on empires that have less than 50,000 acres of land.

Q: What should I do if an empire is continuously attacking me more than 7 times?
A: Be diplomatic and send them a message to see if you can work things out. If that fails and you feel like
you are being stalked, contact an administrator and ask to have the attack logs reviewed.

Q: People keep attacking and taking my land what should I do?
A: When you are not doing a run (playing turns), your land serves little purpose. Land will change hands, that
is part of the game. They attack you, you attack them. Its a simple exchange of land and it is harmless.

Q: When I try to attack someone my generals refuse to attack. Why?
A: If your net is more, or less than 2.5x your opponenents net you will not be able to attack.
If you are both in clans and your clan is set to war this number is increased.

Q: Every time I attack someone part of my army deserts me for attacking a weaker/stronger empire. Why?
A: If the empire name of your opponent is yellow, attacking them will cause you to suffer desertion for attacks on
a much stronger empire, and shame for attacks on a much weaker empire.
If it's an offensive spell only wizards will desert and if it is a regular attack only your troops will desert.

Q: I tried to Aid a friend but it said he was out of my range. What is my Aid range?
A: Greater than 2.5x your net and you will be unable to Aid. There is no limit to how low of an empire you can Aid.

Q: My health never gets to 100% no matter how many turns I spend farming/trading etc... Why?
A: It sounds like your tax rate is set above 11%.

Buildings

Tents
Tents allow you to have more peasants. These should only be used if you are running a casher strat. (see strategy section)

Markets
Markets do 3 things, first they allow you to fit more cash in your bank, they give you a little money per turn, and
they also raise your PCI (per capita income). The amount your PCI is raised depends on the percentage of your land
in markets. I.E. 3,000 markets on 10,000 land will raise your PCI much more than 3,000 markets on 20,000 land.
Markets should only be used in a casher strat.

Camps
Keeps do 3 things also. They lower troop upkeep, lower price of items on the black market, and increase the rate at
which the black market refills. (see Black Market) Camps DO NOT affect market refresh. Camps do not lower upkeep
enough to make a noticeable difference. The real strength of camps lies in their ability to lower Black Market prices.
Camps will give you a maximum discount of 30% off Black Market prices, and you get this max discount when at least
38% of your land is in Camps.

Camps are necessary if you are trying to run a reseller strat (see strat section) or they are also sometimes used
when an empire decides to buy up. 50 turns building Camps can save you 30% more so if you are planning on buying a
lot of troops you might want to consider building some tents right before.

Farms
Produce food for your empire. Used by most all strategies except a few. Having a few farms is never a bad idea.

Mage Towers
Mage Towers make both mages and runes. They will produce mages until you have at least 100 mages per tower. If you
ever have more than 175 mages per tower then a percentage of your mages will die every turn until you get back
down to 175 per tower. The less full your Mage Towers are the faster you gain mages. If you have 40 mages per tower
you will gain mages much faster than you will when you have 80 mages per tower.

All strats need to have some Mage Towers. Bare minimum is enough to make you sufficient runes to cast the spells
raise defenses and prepare troops.

Barracks
Barracks produce troops for your empire. You can change the percentage of each troop built in the manage section.

Guard Towers
A favorite of new players, Guard Towers give you 600 defensive points each. In order to get the full use out of your
guard towers you need at least 100 infantry per tower or they will not be calculated in the defense when someone
attacks you. Some players like the guard towers and some don't. You will need to experiment and see what suits you.


Basic Strategies
This is helpful for a new player just starting out or as a reminder of the basic % of builds recommended.  This is just a basic outline of  strategies.  As you become a better player and gain experience you will learn to tweak your strategy to your unique playing style.
Casher:

Recommended Race: Ogre

Recommended Build:
10% Farms
10% MT
10% Markets
70% Tents
Tax Rate: 30 - 60%

What to do when running: (basic)
Attack for land, build out and spend the rest of your run trading. When your done, buy troops off the black market.

Heroes Recommended:
War Hero - Cregga or Matthias
Peace Hero - Bella
Special Hero - Brome

That's really all there is to it, its a very easy strat and can become one of the most powerful.
Farmer:

Recommended Race: Ent

Recommended Build:
10% Barracks
70% Farms
20% MT
Tax Rate: 11%

What to do when running: (basic)
Attack for land, build out and spend the rest of your run farming. When done sell as much food as you can on Public Market, then sell the rest on the Black Market. Buy troops!

Details:
After doing all your attacking and you have your land grab done, it helps to store all the troops you can on the public market BEFORE you build. This will reduce your cost per turn to maintain your empire and make building cheaper. It leaves you a little open to attack at first, but if done correctly you will eventually have a rotation of troops that can be pulled from the market at any given time. This is really important! Now before your next run, pull all troops off the public market, attack and repeat! Congrats, you now have the knowledge. Go forth and smite those who stand in your way.

Heroes Recommended:
War Hero - choose either attack or defense
Peace Hero - Grumm - 50% more food
Special Hero - Brome - better healing
Mager:

Recommended Race: Orc, Ent, Drow, or Centaur

Recommended Build:
20% Farms
80% MT
Tax Rate: 11%

What to do when running: (basic)
Attack for land, build out and spend the rest of your turns either writing for runes or looting for cash. When done buy all your troops from the black market.

The recommended build has 20% farms because food is a magers weak point, magers can make ALOT of cash but the grain spell doesnt get much at all. So magers usually buy food off the public market, with 20% farms you can usually stay green on food so you only have to watch your cash level.

Heroes Recommended:
War Hero - Mactalon or Cregga
Peace Hero - Methuselah
Special Hero - Brome
Indy:

Recommended Race: Dwarf, Minotaur, Vampire, Goblin, or Golem

Recommended Build:
10% Farms
10% MT
10% Tents
70% Barracks
Tax Rate: 11%

What to do when running: (basic)
Attack for land, build out and spend the rest of your run recruiting. The key is to build one troop type at a time then once you have a good amount of that troop, switch to the next troop type and do it again.

Heroes Recommended:
War Hero - Cregga or Matthias
Peace Hero - Bella
Special Hero - Brome

You must really keep an eye on your resources with this strat as you can quicky run out of cash or food and ruin your empire.
